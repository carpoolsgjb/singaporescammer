Post-Election 2025: Real Questions for the Real Rulers

Singaporeans deserve answers — not silence, not excuses.

Questions PAP Must Answer

  • 1. Why has no one in PAP taken responsibility for the ACRA data leak? Over 800,000 directors' private details exposed — and not a single sacking?
  • 2. How is it that a lie from Raeesah Khan leads to a nationwide media circus, but an ACRA data breach is met with silence? Where’s the justice?
  • 3. Data of hundreds of thousands leaked under your watch — who has been held accountable? Where’s the transparency you always preach?
  • 4. You made Workers' Party pay the price for one person's mistake. Why is the same standard not applied to your own agencies?
  • 5. How is a personal lie worse than a massive breach of national privacy? Which one truly affects more lives?
  • 6. Where is the public report on the ACRA leak investigation? We demand to know what went wrong, and who is being held responsible.
  • 7. Is ACRA’s incompetence not a bigger threat than a backbencher’s speech? Why the double standard, PAP?
  • 8. If opposition parties must answer for every single word, why does PAP get to escape accountability over real, tangible failures?
  • 9. How can Singaporeans trust government data security when not even ministers comment on serious breaches like this?
  • 10. Enough cover-ups. The people deserve answers on ACRA. Start treating your own agencies the way you treat opposition MPs.
  • Now, on Iswaran’s case:
  • 11. Isn’t it the law that public servants who commit crimes should be held to a higher standard — not get lighter punishments?
  • 12. How does a man who accepted over $380,000 in bribes get only 12 months jail — and eligible for home detention after 4 months? Is this justice or privilege?
  • 13. If a hawker stole $10,000 from the government, would he be allowed to go home and rest on his sofa by month 4?
  • 14. Why are penalties harsher for the common man but gentler for ex-ministers with million-dollar salaries?
  • 15. Does being in the cabinet automatically come with immunity perks — lighter jail, home rest, no disgrace?
  • 16. What message are you sending to Singaporeans? That the higher you go in politics, the softer your landing when you fall?
  • 17. Is this the same “clean and fair system” that PAP always claims? Or is there a hidden VIP lane in our justice system?
  • 18. If opposition MPs were found guilty of corruption, would they also get to sleep at home by the 4th month? Or would they be paraded and punished harder?
  • 19. How does a man who betrayed public trust walk away without a full public apology or real consequences?
  • 20. Is Singapore truly a meritocracy — or a hierarchy of selective justice, where your punishment depends on your connections?
